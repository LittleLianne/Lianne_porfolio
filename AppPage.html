The following project is an app that allows users to keep track of endometriosis symptoms as well as communicate with other users of the app.

The approach >>>

1.Research

At the start of the project, research was conducted to research existing ways of tracking endometriosis symptoms.

2.Identify any issues.

Issues had to be identified at the start of the project. I had to make sure all issues were documented and understood.

3.Early stage questionnaires.

This stage allowed me to gather more information on how endometriosis symptoms were currently tracked and allowed me to gather information about potential users of the app.

4.Personas.

The personas allowed me to describe an individual typical user of the app. It provided a means for focusing on the userâ€™s needs and requirements.

5.User Profile.

The user profile described the attributes and characteristics of potential users.

6.Literature Reviews.

Throughout the project literature review was done, and some of the sources of literature that I researched were designing for an interface, human-centred design and different books on endometriosis.

7.Needs and Requirements.

Needs and requirements are essential to any interactive product as needs need to be met and understood.

8.Use Cases and Essential Use Cases.

This helped decided what tasks the users could perform on the app and the design elements.

9.Storyboard.

The storyboard helped explore concepts, context, workflow and it helped to investigate the environment where the app would be used.

10.Scenarios.

Written description of the storyboard.

11.Create all the materials needed for the evaluation.

Interface sketches, card based prototypes, interview scripts, questionnaires, consent forms, task cards and data collection form.

12.Interface Sketches.

Low fidelity prototypes, I created two interface sketch the main one and an alternative one.

13.Card Based Prototypes.

These worked well alongside the interface sketches.

14.Interview Scripts.

The interview script was created to ensure all the participants at the evaluation were told the same information and that all correct information was told.

15.Questionnaires.

Pre-questions and after evaluation of the interface sketch questions were created with a mixture of both open and closed questions used.

16.Consent Forms.

Consent forms were created to ensure participants agree to take part in the evaluation as well as photos been taken, it also provided information on the evaluation its self as well as informing the participants that their names would not be identified at any time and that they could withdraw from the evaluation at any time if they needed to.

17.Task Cards.

Task cards were cards that have different tasks on them for the participants to do.

18.Data Collection Forms.

By using a data collection form it allowed me to note any comments made by participants during the evaluation and it also allowed me to note any observer notes down.

19.Evaluation.

The evaluation allowed me to check that users could interact with the interface sketches and to see if it meet the needs and requirements.

20.Analysis the data/summarize the finding.

The data was analysed and summarized which resulted in three majors changes to the interface been needed.

21.Heuristic Evaluation.

To do this evaluation Nielsen list of heuristic was used.

22.The final design was created using wire-framing.
